Get to Know Me

Hi, I’m Lex.

I don’t know how to love in moderation. I wasn’t taught to care with restraints, and I’ve tried to soften my love just to make it easier for others to hold, but that’s not me and it never was.

So sometimes I choose distance not because I stopped loving, but because I couldn’t love less. Staying would feel like betraying what I genuinely feel.

My love doesn’t demand anything. It doesn’t chase, corner, or ask to be chosen back. It stays soft even when it hurts, gives without keeping score, and knows when to let go without turning bitter.

At the same time, I know I can be distant. Not because someone did something wrong, and not because I’m mad. I just don’t always feel the need to talk or stay connected all the time.

Maybe I’m too much sometimes too soft, too open, too loud but I’ve learned that dimming myself never made anyone stay longer.

So I’d rather be real, messy but wholehearted, than be someone who feels nothing at all.

And on quiet days, I find myself wishing I could tell my father everything that’s happened in my life.

I miss that kind of comfort — being able to talk, to be understood, to just sit there and feel safe. It’s something I still long for in my own way.
